Hierarchical Autoregressive Modeling with Multi-Scale Refinement for Robot Policy Learning

Anonymous RA-L Submission

Abstract

Autoregressive mechanisms have driven milestone advancements in text and image generation. However, directly applying autoregressive paradigms to robotic policy faces critical challenges including weak holistic comprehension, significant cumulative errors, and limited multi-modal modeling ability.

In this paper, we propose HAMR, a novel hierarchical autoregressive paradigm with a multi-scale action representation mechanism, enabling efficient and accurate action prediction. Specifically, HAMR employs a dual-autoregressive architecture: a high-level module focuses on capturing long-horizon motion features, while a low-level module generates fine-grained actions, such that enhancing the model's general understanding ability. Within the low-level module, a multi-scale action prediction scheme encodes trajectories from coarse to fine. This mechanism utilizes fine-grained feature outputs to correct coarse predictions to reduce cumulative errors. Building upon this, we propose a lightweight action decoder based on diffusion-loss that reconstructs these multi-scale features into precise actions, improving multi-modal modeling ability while maintaining high computational efficiency. Experiments demonstrate HAMR's exceptional performance across over 50 tasks, outperforming state-of-the-art methods by 17% and 23% in simulated and real-world environments respectively.
