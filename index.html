Hierarchical Autoregressive Modeling with Multi-Scale Refinement for Robot Policy Learning

Anonymous RA-L Submission

Abstract

Autoregressive mechanisms have driven milestone advancements in text and image generation. However, autoregressive-based robot policies still face critical challenges including weak holistic comprehension, significant cumulative errors, and limited multi-modal modeling ability. In this paper, we propose HAMR, a novel Hierarchical Autoregressive paradigm with Multi-scale action Refinement, enabling efficient and accurate action prediction. Specifically, HAMR employs a hierarchical architecture: a high-level module focuses on capturing long-horizon motion features, while a low-level module generates fine-grained actions, thereby enhancing the model's general understanding ability. Within the low-level module, a multi-scale action prediction scheme encodes trajectories from coarse to fine. This mechanism utilizes fine-grained feature outputs to correct coarse predictions, reducing cumulative errors. Building upon this, we propose a lightweight action decoder based on diffusion-loss that reconstructs these multi-scale features into precise actions, improving multi-modal modeling ability while maintaining high computational efficiency. Experiments demonstrate HAMR's exceptional performance across over 60 tasks, outperforming state-of-the-art methods by 12% and 17% in simulated and real-world environments respectively.
